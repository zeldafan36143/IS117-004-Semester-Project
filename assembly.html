Parts Assembly

Once I have all the parts, what do I do?

Building a custom gaming computer can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and knowledge, it can be a fun and rewarding experience. The basic parts you will need to build a gaming PC include a graphics card, motherboard, CPU, RAM, power supply, storage, and case. Once you have all of the necessary components, you can begin assembling your computer.

First, you should start by preparing your workspace with all the necessary tools, such as screwdrivers and zip ties. Then, you can begin by installing the CPU onto the motherboard, making sure to match the notches on the chip with the socket on the board. Then install the CPU cooler onto the motherboard, on top of the CPU. Next, install the RAM into the slots on the motherboard, making sure they click into place.

After that, you can mount the motherboard into the case, aligning the screw holes with those on the case. Secure the motherboard in place with screws, being careful not to overtighten them. Then, install the power supply into the case, making sure it is mounted securely.

Next, you can connect the necessary cables from the power supply to the motherboard, including the main 24-pin ATX cable and the CPU ATX power cable. Then, connect the storage devices, such as hard drives or solid-state drives, to the motherboard using the appropriate cables.

Finally, you can install the graphics card into the appropriate PCIe slot on the motherboard and connect it to the power supply using the appropriate cables. Once all of the components are connected and secured, you can close the case and power on the computer to make sure everything is working properly.

Remember to consult the manuals for each component for specific instructions and take your time during the assembly process to avoid damaging any components. Good luck with your custom gaming build!
