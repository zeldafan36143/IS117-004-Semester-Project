Troubleshooting

What do I do if something goes wrong?

Building your own PC can be a rewarding experience, but it can also come with its own set of challenges. When troubleshooting a newly built computer, there are several common issues to look out for. First, it's important to check for heat issues and make sure all system fans are running. If the computer is experiencing performance issues or crashes, it may be due to overheating components. Additionally, it's important to ensure that all power connections, including the main power and ATX12V connector, are firmly attached.

If the computer is not booting up properly, one common issue may be improperly installed RAM. Try reseating the RAM modules to ensure they are installed correctly. Similarly, an improperly installed processor can also cause issues. Check the surrounding area for damage, such as bent pins in the socket or CPU, and ensure that the cooler is properly attached.

Another common issue with newly built computers is connectivity issues with peripherals. If the keyboard or mouse is not working, check to make sure it's correctly plugged in or that wireless devices are turned on and have charged batteries. If the sound isn't working, check the volume level and ensure that the speakers or headphones are properly connected.

If none of these solutions work, it may be necessary to check for BIOS updates or to try rolling back GPU drivers. It's also important to keep calm and not get frustrated. Troubleshooting a newly built computer can be a time-consuming process, but with patience and persistence, most issues can be resolved.
